DEFINITION

BACK

CUBE

A three-dimensional shape with six square sides all the same size

CUBOID

A box-shaped solid with three rectangular faces, like a rectangular box or book.

RIGHT CIRCULAR CYLINDER

A three-dimensional shape with circular bases at both ends, connected by a straight, upright side.

SPHERE

A perfectly round, three-dimensional shape where all points on its surface are the same distance from its center.

HEMISPHERE

A half of a sphere, like a ball cut in two

RIGHT PRISH

A 3D shape with flat parallel bases connected by rectangular sides.

RIGHT PYRAMID

A base directly beneath its pointed top.

SURFACE AREA

the total amount of space covering the outside of a three-dimensional object.

VOLUME

The amount of space occupied by a three-dimensional object.

LENGTH

Refers to the distance between two points on a straight line.

BASE

A flat surface, a 3D figure rests on, or a side of a 2D shape used to calculate area.

HEIGHT

Refers to the perpendicular distance between a vertex and its opposite side in a shape

PI

A number roughly equal to 3.14 that represents the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.

RADIUS

Refers to the distance from the center of a circle or sphere to its edge.

SIDE LENGTH

Refers to the constant length of any of its edges
