TRIANGLE

Refers to a flat, three-sided polygon with three angles that add up to 180 degrees.

HEIGHT

Refers to the perpendicular distance from a vertex to the opposite side (its base).

BASE

In a triangle, the base is any of the three sides that you choose, as long as the height is the perpendicular line drawn from the opposite vertex to that base.

AREA OF A TRIANGLE

The area of a triangle represents the amount of flat space enclosed by its three sides, like the amount of paint needed to cover a triangular sail.

PERIMETER OF A TRIANGLE

Refers to the total length of all its sides added together.

EQUILATERAL TRIANGLE

An equilateral triangle is a three-sided shape where all three sides are the same length.

ISOSCELES TRIANGLE

An isosceles triangle has at least two sides that are the same length.

PYTHAGOREAN THEOREM

In a right triangle, a² + b² = c², where c is the longest side (hypotenuse) and a & b are the other two sides.

LEG

In a right triangle, the legs are the two shorter sides that meet at the right angle.

HYPOTENUSE

The hypotenuse is the longest side of a right triangle, opposite the right angle itself.
