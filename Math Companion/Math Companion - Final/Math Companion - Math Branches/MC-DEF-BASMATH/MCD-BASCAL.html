ARITHMETIC SEQUENCE

An arithmetic sequence is a list of numbers where the difference between consecutive terms is constant.

COMMON DIFFERENCE

In an arithmetic sequence, the common difference is the constant value you add (or subtract) to go from one term to the next.

GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE

A list of numbers where you multiply by a constant value to get from one term to the next.

FACTORIAL

The factorial of a number is the product of all positive integers less than or equal to that number.

FRACTION

A fraction represents a part of a whole, written as two numbers with a slash separating them (e.g., 1/2).

IMPROPER FRACTION

An improper fraction is a fraction where the whole thing (numerator) is equal to or bigger than the number of pieces (denominator).

MIXED FRACTION

A mixed fraction is a number that combines a whole number and a proper fraction.

DECIMAL

A decimal is a number that combines a whole number and a fractional part, written with a decimal point separating the two.
